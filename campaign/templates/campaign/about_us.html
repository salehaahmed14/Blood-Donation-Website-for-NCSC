About Us

NUST Community Services Club (NCSC) is a non-profit and student-run, volunteer organization established for the welfare of society. It is the platform where students of NUST unite to pursue their altruistic endeavors of serving humanity and giving back to their community. We always strive for inclusion and empathy; and thus we make sure that our services and efforts are accessible to all regardless of their ethnicity, religion, disability and gender. NCSC, as it is known today, was founded on December 27, 2007, by Dr. Ijaz Ahmed Qureshi (Ex. Assistant Professor, Department of Computing – SEECS ). It is currently under the patronage of the School of Mechanical and Manufacturing Engineering (SMME), supervised by faculty sponsor Dr. Muhammad Safdar and facilitated by assistant managers, Maheen Mumtaz and Sehrish Hamid. In addition to inspiring young people to join community service, one of NCSC’s greatest objectives is to bring out and enhance leadership skills in its members. Each member of NCSC has the opportunity to lead a team and engage in the successful planning, execution, and completion of numerous events and projects.
